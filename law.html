Laws & Regulations

Explore key health policies and regulatory frameworks guiding HHS services.

HIPAA - Privacy Rule

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act protects patient privacy and secures health information.

Read More

HIPAA ensures the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of health information. Healthcare providers must comply with security rules and inform patients about their rights.

Affordable Care Act (ACA)

The ACA expands access to healthcare, improves quality, and reduces healthcare costs across the U.S.

Read More

The ACA includes protections for people with preexisting conditions, subsidies for low-income individuals, and the individual mandate requiring insurance coverage.

Medicare & Medicaid Regulations

These rules govern the eligibility, benefits, and delivery of care under federal health insurance programs.

Read More

Medicare serves older adults and people with disabilities, while Medicaid offers support for low-income individuals. Each has different requirements and covered services.

Food & Drug Safety

FDA regulations ensure the safety and efficacy of drugs, medical devices, and food products in the U.S.

Read More

All manufacturers must follow strict testing, labeling, and approval procedures. The FDA also monitors post-market safety and responds to health risks.

Public Health Emergency Preparedness

Legal frameworks for managing health crises, pandemics, and bioterrorism responses.

Read More

HHS coordinates with local, state, and federal agencies to ensure readiness, resource allocation, and communication during national health emergencies.
